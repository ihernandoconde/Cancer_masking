What are BI-RADS?

Breast cancer is [this data], a common risk factor is breast density which is measured in a scale called BI-RADS, this may be either A, B, C, or D. This works because the proportion of fat to non-fat areas in the breast may be an indication of risk of breast cancer. Denser breast are more likely to develop cancer than less dense ones. Another risk factor is different density results between both breasts.

Calculate your BI-RADS here

By uploading the image of your mammograms, we can estimate the BI-RADS(Breast Imaging Reporting and Database System) score you may have in terms of breast density. As explained above, this may be either A, B, C, or D, but it only indicates possible risk as is not to be taken in a diagnostic capacity. We will also output the density for the main four areas for more localized analysis. If you want to check out the symmetry of results between both breasts, upload a second mammogram.

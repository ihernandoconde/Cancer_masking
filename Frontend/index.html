Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is the most frequently diagnosed cancer in women worldwide. It is estimated that 1 in 8 women will develop breast cancer during their lifetime. With early detection, survival rates exceeds 90% so regular screening is essential if possible. Because of healthcare access disparity, diagnostics remain a challenge in low-resource regions so using our software you can upload you screening results to understand your risk better.

Risk Factors

Breast density is a main risk factor for breast cancer development. Dense breast tissue consists of more fibrous tissue instead of fatty tissue and it makes it harder to identify abnormalities when screening. Higher breast density makes women 4 to 6 times more likely to develop breast cancer, and it hides tumours on imaging, delaying diagnosis. Another risk factor is asymmetry in breast density between both breasts, which occurs when results from both breast have a noticeable difference, this can be an early sign of developing malignancy.

What are BI-RADS?

The BI-RADS scale helps radiologists and doctors classify findings from breast imaging and identify risk levels for developing breast cancers. It ranges from 1 to 4, BI-RADS 1 meaning normal findings while BI-RADS 4 suggests abnormal results that might require a further testing. This systems includes breast density categories (A to D) which depend on density results, a risk factor of breast cancer. In this scale, A being lower density and D being highest, as seen below.
